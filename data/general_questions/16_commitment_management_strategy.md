# Commitment Management Strategy

## Purpose
Guide decisions about commitments such as savings plans or reservations.

## Executive objective
Lower effective rates without creating unnecessary lock-in or forecasting risk.

## Best practices
- buy commitments only after allocation, forecast, and usage visibility are sufficiently reliable
- separate steady-state baseline usage from volatile usage
- review coverage and utilization regularly
- align commitment terms with business confidence and demand stability
- document downside risk if workloads shrink, migrate, or change architecture

## CFO questions this document supports
- Are we under-committed and paying on-demand rates unnecessarily?
- Are we over-committed and carrying waste risk?
- Which workloads are stable enough for commitments?
- What governance should exist before purchase approval?

## Decision criteria
- stability of baseline usage
- confidence in forecast
- business criticality
- flexibility needs
- expected savings vs downside risk
