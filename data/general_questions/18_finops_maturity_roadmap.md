# FinOps Maturity Roadmap

## Purpose

Provide a realistic roadmap for improving FinOps capability over time.

## Stage 1: Visibility and ownership

Focus:

* cost and usage ingestion
* allocation basics
* tag policy
* reporting
* anomaly alerts
Outcome:
* leaders can explain major spend changes and assign owners

## Stage 2: Financial discipline

Focus:

* monthly budgets
* rolling forecasts
* variance analysis
* commitment review process
Outcome:
* finance and engineering can anticipate overspend and take earlier action

## Stage 3: Value optimization

Focus:

* unit economics
* workload optimization
* architecture trade-offs
* portfolio prioritization
Outcome:
* spend decisions are tied to business impact rather than cost alone

## Stage 4: Scaled practice

Focus:

* policy automation
* exception workflows
* standardized playbooks
* mature operating cadence
Outcome:
* FinOps becomes repeatable across teams and products

## Executive note

A CFO should expect progression from visibility to discipline to value. Skipping directly to advanced optimization without ownership and allocation quality usually fails.

\## FinOps Maturity Stages



Common FinOps maturity stages include:

1\. Visibility – understanding cloud usage and cost

2\. Optimization – improving efficiency and reducing waste

3\. Governance – implementing financial controls and accountability

4\. Advanced – aligning cost with business value and scaling FinOps practices
