# CFO FAQ for FinOps

## Purpose
Provide fast answers to broad executive questions.

## Question
What is FinOps in simple terms?

## Answer
FinOps is a cross-functional operating practice that helps an organization understand technology spend, connect it to business value, and make timely decisions about cost, usage, and optimization.

## Question
Is FinOps mainly about cutting cost?

## Answer
No. It is about maximizing business value. Cost reduction is one possible outcome, but the better question is whether spend is justified, visible, and aligned to outcomes.

## Question
What should a CFO ask every month?

## Answer
- Where did spend change the most?
- Which changes were expected versus unexpected?
- What is budget variance by major scope?
- How accurate is the latest forecast?
- Which teams cannot explain their deltas?
- What is cost per key business metric?
- Which actions are pending and what savings or value impact are expected?

## Question
How many KPIs should leadership review?

## Answer
Keep the executive view small and stable: spend, forecast variance, budget variance, allocation coverage, tag compliance, anomaly count and resolution time, and one or two unit economics metrics.

## Question
What is the first sign that FinOps is weak?

## Answer
Teams cannot explain spend changes in business terms, ownership is unclear, and finance learns about overruns too late.

## Question
Should every team own cloud cost?

## Answer
Yes, but with support. Shared accountability means Finance, Engineering, Product, and FinOps each own a part of the decision loop.

## Question
When should we increase spend instead of optimize it down?

## Answer
When the additional spend supports strategic growth, improves resilience, accelerates delivery, or lowers a larger risk or operating cost elsewhere.

## Question
What is a practical maturity path?

## Answer
Start with visibility and ownership, then improve forecast and budget discipline, then scale optimization and unit economics, then automate policy and decision workflows.
