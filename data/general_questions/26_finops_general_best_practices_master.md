# FinOps General Best Practices Master Guide

## Purpose
Answer broad executive questions about what "good FinOps" looks like in practice.

## Best practice pillars
### 1. Visibility before optimization
Organizations should first make cost data accessible, timely, accurate, and understandable.

### 2. Ownership by scope
Every meaningful spend segment should have an accountable owner.

### 3. Finance and engineering collaboration
Financial accountability cannot be delegated to finance alone or engineering alone.

### 4. Forecasting as a management habit
Forecasting is not a once-a-quarter exercise. It should be part of routine operating discipline.

### 5. Optimization tied to business value
The goal is not minimum spend. The goal is the best business value from technology spend.

### 6. Central enablement with distributed action
A central FinOps function should define standards, while product and platform teams take local action.

### 7. Progressive maturity
Start with a narrow scope, prove value, then expand in scale and complexity.

## Common failure modes
- poor allocation and missing tags
- budgets without owners
- noisy alerts with no response process
- commitment purchases without stable demand
- optimization tracked as savings theater rather than realized value
- no linkage between cloud cost and business outcomes

## CFO answer shortcut
When asked for the most important best practices:
1. establish visibility
2. define accountable scopes
3. implement forecast and budget discipline
4. govern commitments carefully
5. review optimization as a recurring operating process
6. connect spend to business value
