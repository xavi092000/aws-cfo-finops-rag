# FinOps Domains and Capabilities

## Purpose
This document organizes the major FinOps outcome areas and the capabilities that support them.

## Domain: Understand Usage & Cost
### Goal
Create visibility into what is being used, by whom, and at what cost.

### Capabilities
- Data Ingestion
- Allocation
- Reporting & Analytics
- Anomaly Management

### Example AWS interpretation
- ingest AWS CUR data
- allocate cost with tags and account mappings
- build cost dashboards
- detect unusual cost spikes

## Domain: Quantify Business Value
### Goal
Connect spend to planning, forecasting, budgets, benchmarks, and business outcomes.

### Capabilities
- Planning & Estimating
- Forecasting
- Budgeting
- KPIs & Benchmarking
- Unit Economics

### Example AWS interpretation
- estimate monthly run rate
- compare actuals versus budget
- track cost per product, dashboard, pipeline, or customer cohort

## Domain: Optimize Usage & Cost
### Goal
Improve technology efficiency without losing needed business value.

### Capabilities
- Architecting & Workload Placement
- Usage Optimization
- Rate Optimization
- Licensing & SaaS
- Sustainability

### Example AWS interpretation
- rightsize EC2
- optimize S3 storage class usage
- review Redshift sizing and pause patterns
- improve commitment coverage strategy

## Domain: Manage the FinOps Practice
### Goal
Run FinOps as an operating model, not as a one-off reporting exercise.

### Capabilities
- Executive Strategy Alignment
- FinOps Practice Operations
- Governance, Policy & Risk
- FinOps Education & Enablement
- Invoicing & Chargeback
- FinOps Assessment
- Automation, Tools & Services
- Intersecting Disciplines

### Example AWS interpretation
- define monthly review cadence
- establish policy for untagged resources
- automate anomaly alerts and remediation suggestions
