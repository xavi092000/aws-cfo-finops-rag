# FinOps Personas

## Purpose
This document lists the main personas involved in a FinOps practice and clarifies their roles in an AWS-focused organization.

## Core personas
### FinOps Practitioner
Coordinates the practice, translates between teams, defines standards, and drives reporting, education, and optimization workflows.

### Engineering
Owns resource usage, architecture choices, deployment patterns, and service-level cost drivers.

### Finance
Validates reporting, supports budgeting and forecasting, and helps align cost visibility to financial planning.

### Leadership
Sets business priorities, approves targets, and evaluates trade-offs between performance, resilience, and spend.

### Procurement
Supports negotiations, commitment planning, and vendor management.

### Product
Connects cloud spend to product value, usage patterns, and customer outcomes.

## Allied personas
### IT Asset Management
Helps align software, licensing, and asset tracking.

### IT Financial Management
Supports financial governance and cost planning.

### IT Service Management
Connects service operations to usage and cost behavior.

### Security
Evaluates controls, risk posture, and compliance-related cost impact.

### Sustainability
Connects optimization efforts to environmental impact where relevant.

## AWS mapping examples
- Engineering: EC2 rightsizing, Redshift cluster design, data pipeline scheduling
- Finance: monthly variance analysis, forecast review
- Leadership: budget thresholds, target unit economics
- Procurement: Savings Plans and Reserved Instance coverage strategy

## Typical questions this document helps answer
- Who should act on a cost anomaly?
- Which team owns rate optimization?
- Why must finance and engineering both be involved?
