# Showback and Chargeback Policy

## Purpose
Explain how technology costs are communicated and allocated to business owners.

## Definitions
### Showback
Provide cost visibility to teams without directly billing them.

### Chargeback
Allocate and assign costs to teams, products, or business units for financial accountability.

## When to use each model
- Use showback first when organizational maturity is low or allocation quality is still improving.
- Use chargeback when ownership is accepted, allocation rules are stable, and the business needs tighter accountability.

## Policy principles
- Allocation rules must be transparent and documented.
- Shared costs need a defined allocation basis.
- Unallocated spend must be tracked and reduced over time.
- Business owners should be able to understand and challenge allocation logic.

## Allocation bases
- direct tag-based allocation
- usage-based allocation
- proportional allocation by agreed driver such as active users, transactions, or storage volume
- fixed split only when no better driver exists

## Executive guidance
For a CFO audience, showback is often the fastest route to behavior change. Chargeback is stronger, but it should not be introduced before ownership, data quality, and escalation paths are clear.
